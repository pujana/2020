Call for Games Industry talks

The IEEE Conference on Games will be hosting a day of industry talks on 24th August 2020 at Kindai University, Japan. This industry day will include a number of keynotes and industry talks. The organisers invite talk proposals from members of the games industry, interested in engaging with game developers and researchers. We are looking forward to submissions from all over the community. This includes tools, best practices, demonstrations and the latest developments from various fields and backgrounds within the games industry. For your reference, please see last year industry day program.

Each industry talk has a time slot of 20 minutes (15 minutes for talk + 5 minutes for Q&A) plus a demo if desired during the demo session on the same day and should focus on a technical contribution to a game in one or more of the conference topic areas:

  • AI for Playing Games
  • Analytics and Player Modelling
  • Benchmarks and Competitions
  • Education and Games
  • Game Design
  • Game Interfaces and User Interaction
  • Game Theory and Multi-agent Systems
  • Multimedia and Games
  • Narrative and Interactive Entertainment
  • PCG and AI for Game Design
  • Serious Games Technology
  • Virtual and Augmented Reality

We also welcome proposals for 'Getting into the industry' talks by established game professionals.

Attendance to the Industry day is free. Speakers of the accepted talks have a discounted fee to attend the rest of the conference.

IMPORTANT DATES

  • 12th June 2020 – Deadline to submit talk proposals
  • 26th June 2020 – Speaker notification of talks accepted
  • 10th August 2020 - Deadline for slides of accepted talks
  • 24th August 2020 – Industry day at COG (date of talk)
