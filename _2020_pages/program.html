Conference program

We plan to have a number of events as listed below. Events that form the core of the conference are described next:

  • Regular Paper Sessions: Paper sessions will be determined by the type and areas
    • of the papers accepted. A best paper session will be allocated with those papers that received the highest score in the peer review process. These sessions will take place in the multi-purpose hall. If needed, parallel sessions can be held in rooms called Room-A. These sessions form the core of the conference schedule.
  • Competition Session: As in previous CIG conferences, multiple competitions will be
    • held and results of those will be presented in a session (plenary or parallel, depending on the number of competitions organized).
  • Poster and Demonstration session: Poster presentations and technical demos will
    • be held in the multi-purpose hall.
  • Keynote talks: We plan to invite three keynote speakers for the last 3 days of the
    • conference. The keynote talks will be plenary and held in the multi-purpose hall.
  • Industry Session: This session will count on speakers from the game industry that
    • present their work to the audience. There will be a call for talk proposals. Once their talk proposals are accepted, these speakers are charged reduced registration fees but do not submit a paper for inclusion in the proceedings. The objective of this session is two-fold: i) we expect to bring game industry professionals to the conference to attend high quality talks (who would pay normal registration fees) and ii) promote a higher collaboration between the fields of game academic and industry.
  • Panel/Open mic Sessions: In order to promote this last point, we will hold a panel
    • with experts, followed by an open mic session, designed to find collaborations and identify industry needs.
  • Tutorial Sessions: Several tutorials will be delivered on the first day of the
    • conference, to be held in the multi-purpose hall. If needed, parallel sessions can also be held in Room-As.

Additionally, the following social events are scheduled.

  • The Welcome Party will be held at the LOUNGE in the academic theater on the main campus, where is within 5 minutes walking distance from the conference venue, on the first evening.
  • The Social Evening will be held on the second day, we will take the participants to Namba area. In this area, three big game-centers are located within 100 meters, which are operated by major Japanese arcade game companies: BANDAI NAMCO, SEGA and Taito.
  • The Banquet and Award Ceremony will be held at the Swissotel Nankai, where is within 7 minutes walking distance from the game centers, on the third day of the conference.

A preliminary schedule for the 4 days proposed is included next:

  • Monday 24th August:
    • Registration
    • Tutorials
    • Social event: Welcome Party
  • Tuesday 25th August:
    • Keynote 1
    • Regular paper sessions
    • Poster and Demo session
    • Social Event: Social Evening
  • Wednesday 26th August:
    • Keynote 2
    • Regular paper sessions
    • Industry session (industry talks, panel and open mic)
    • Social Event: Banquet and Award Ceremony
  • Thursday 27th August:
    • Keynote 3
    • Regular paper sessions
    • Competition session
    • Farewell and Announcements
